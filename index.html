A canvas where nineteenth-century history meets contemporary vision.

The Residence

Set high above the water to command uninterrupted views of Puget Sound and the Narrows Bridge beyond, the ready-to-build 6,200 sq ft primary residence is a study in quiet confidence — architecture that steps back so the horizon can step forward. A luxury pool and private pool house extend the living space outward, blurring the line between interior comfort and island air.

The Collector’s Pavilion

A 2,500 sq ft ready-to-build detached garage — oversized pass-through doors, soaring ceilings — stands ready for a collection worthy of the setting. This is not storage. It is a gallery for the machines you love.

The Post Office

At the heart of the property sits a piece of American history: from 1915 to 1932, the island’s post office, waiting to be reimagined. Boathouse. Salon. Private retreat. Its next purpose is unwritten — reserved for an owner who understands that the rarest luxury is a story no one else can tell.

Cedrona Cove

Below it all, low-bank access opens onto the warm, protected waters of Cedrona Cove — generations of summer afternoons, only beginning to write the next ones, with future dock and pier exploration options.

Fox Island

Reached by a 1954 suspension bridge from the Gig Harbor peninsula, Fox Island sits apart from the pace of the mainland — a few thousand acres of fir forest and rocky shoreline in southern Puget Sound.

Seattle and Tacoma are close enough for a morning meeting, far enough that the island keeps its own weather, its own light, and a shoreline that still feels undiscovered.
